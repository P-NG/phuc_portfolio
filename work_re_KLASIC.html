OVERVIEW

2020 is a year of unprecedented times as the COVID-19 pandemic halts humanity and confine us within our homes. However, life continues on as we adopt the virtual world into our everyday lives from Zoom calls to online shopping. Online shopping is more relevant than ever before so as a designer and fashion enthusiast, I created re_KLASIC, a fictional brick and mortar fashion retailer that heavily relies on online purchases from its web store. The re_KLASIC web store is aesthetically pleasing to browse with filters to help guide shoppers to whatever they're looking for. The goal of re_KLASIC is to create an enjoyable and seamless shopping experience for the average joe to a fashion enthusiast.

Images sourced from SSENSE, HYPEBEAST, END CLOTHING

PROBLEMS

The initial with many online web stores is the clutter of information and selections on the homepage that are redundant. In result, users get overwhelm and deter them to fully scroll through the page to view the selections.

SOLUTIONS

To mitigate the execessive clutter of selections on the homepage, the page is divided into four sections, each with its distinct layout of elements. The hiearchy of the selection is determined by the factors of how relevant and updatable the information is.

UX CHALLENGES

Since re_KLASIC is an online retailer for both men and women fashion, it was initially challenging to design a refinement system that function effortlessly and consistently for both gender’s selections.

GOALS

The goal of re_KLASIC is to showcase that functional minimalism is perhaps more effective and louder at promoting products than forcing everything at the shoppers from the start.

UNDERSTANDING

Words from Frequent Online Shoppers

Starting, I reached out to a number of friends who are fashion enthusiasts to ask them about their pet peeves with online fashion retailers. Fashion enthusiasts being one of the two main demographics of re_KLASIC, I wanted to understand and find solutions to improve their experience with shopping for clothes online. Hearing these pain points I have concluded that re_KLASIC shoppers needed...

INSIGHT 1:

Eliminate unnecessary animations that don't elevate the experience.

INSIGHT 2:

Sizing system for products with visual aids to help shoppers understand the measurements

INSIGHT 3:

Filter with feature to select multiple brands.

PRODUCT ANALYSIS

A product analysis was performed to analyze the distilled methods that were used to create an online fashion retailer. Here are the products that I analyzed.

From the collected information of the user's needs and product analysis, these are the key takeaways I have identified.


1. Simple, streamlined experience is favored.
Users like to simplify their experience with selections that can be refine to specific items. Complicating the initial selecting process with numerous categories only slow down the users before they see any products.

2. Relevance is key.
Users get overwhelm when masses of different information is display in one location without any relevant connection to their goals.

DEFINING THE PROBLEM

After conducting the product analysis and gaining insights from frequent online fashion shoppers, I put together a problem statement that identified the gap between current problem and the goal, "How might we create a simple and inclusive shopping experience?"

REFLECTION

The main takeaway from adapting the re_KLASIC web prototype to mobile is that a successful design must be seamlessly adaptable to all devices. For example, horizontal scroll is not user friendly on all devices such as desktop due to the technical limitation of mouses. Additionally, hovering features are not convertible to mobile design so they must be straightforward in order to stay consistent from desktop to mobile. ​​​​​​​>
